how it works

01
Sign in with Google
One-time OAuth flow. karma reads Gmail metadata only — sender, subject, date. It never reads your message bodies.
02
Grant Full Disk Access
For iMessage, karma reads your local chat.db directly. Requires Full Disk Access in System Settings. Nothing leaves your Mac.
03
It runs quietly
karma fetches every 90 minutes in the background and on wake. No notifications. The menu bar title shows counts when there's something new.
04
Click when you're ready
Open the popover deliberately. Read your brief. Close it. That's the whole interaction — designed to take 15 seconds, not 15 minutes.

the idea

"Information satiety, not information resistance.
The goal isn't to block the world — it's to meet it
on your own schedule."

karma is the Light Phone for your Mac.

what it does and does not do

Gmail
Last 5 emails via the Gmail API. Sender and subject only — no body content, no tracking pixels.
iMessage
Unread messages from your local chat.db. Sender and first line. Processed entirely on device.
No notifications
karma never pushes information at you. The menu bar updates silently. You check it; it doesn't interrupt you.
No badges
No red dots. No unread counts in the Dock. No visual anxiety loops. The title shows counts or "karma." That's it.
No Dock icon
LSUIElement — karma is invisible in the Dock and app switcher. It exists only in the menu bar.
Open source
MIT licensed. Build it yourself or read exactly what it does before you trust it with your data.
